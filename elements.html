New Orleans Pothole Tracker

This dashboard was created for a class titled "Internet GIS/Spatial Databases" at Delta State University. For this dashboard I used ArcGIS Pro, ArcGIS Survey 123, and ArcGIS Dashboards. The goal of this tool was to provide a place where the general public could report potholes and view potholes that were already reported to New Orleans 311. This dashboard also has a view which allows the user to analyze the distribution of potholes based on neighborhood income, this allows the user to determine whether pothole repair is happening equitably across all neighborhoods.

Geospatial Solutions for Locating Mass Graves

Geospatial Solutions for Locating Mass Graves is a 4-part research project I conducted as part of my degree capstone. The first section provided a literature review on the history and recommended geospatial solutions for mass grave investigations. The next two sections examined emerging research trends and covered the utility of geospatial databases in evidence collection and demonstrated workflows for using satellite imagery to detect land changes indicative of mass graves. Each section included detailed summaries, interactive visuals, graphics, and maps to enhance reader engagement. For the conclusion, I analyzed two mass graves reported in April 2024, using building damage data from the United Nations Satellite Centre (UNOSAT) and imagery from the Copernicus Satellite to conduct a time series analysis of land changes. My aim was to illustrate the value of documenting mass graves and the potential of spatial analysis in creating historical records of such events.
